How to cook chicken adobo

By: Caesar Klidge C. Galutera BSCS-IAA2

Ingredients,Substitutions and Adjustments:

  • 1 kilo Chicken thighs - This dish can be made with any type of chicken. I recommend using bone-in chicken thighs since they are juicy.
  • 5 cloves minced Garlic - One of the traditional ingredients in adobo is garlic. It gives the braising liquid a wonderful flavor.
  • 1/4 cup Dark soy sauce - This ingredient gives the chicken a dark brown hue and adds umami flavor. You can use ordinary soy sauce instead, but the color will not be as vibrant. Use tamari as a gluten-free substitute.
  • 1/2 cup White vinegar -White vinegar is used by most adobos. Apple cider vinegar makes a good replacement.
  • 1 teaspoon brown sugar - The sugar's sweetness counteracts the saltiness of the oyster sauce and dark soy sauce.
  • 1 peice Bay leaves and 1 table spoon black pepper - Adds a subtle bitterness to the dish, which helps to balance out the heavier flavors.
  • 1/4 cup Cooking oil - Oil is needed to saute the chicken.
  • 1 tablespoon Salt - To clean the chicken and get rid of its smell before cooking.
  • 1 cup water
    • - water will dilute the flavor, only add it if, for you, is too sweet or too salty.

Procedures on cooking the chicken adobo:

  1. Start by cleaning the chicken by rubbing with salt.
  2. In a pan, heat up the 1/4 cup of cooking oil. When heated, saute the garlic until golden brown.
  3. Saute the chicken until golden brown.
  4. Add the dark soy sauce, white vinegar. Put into boiling. Cover the pan, reduce the heat and simmer for about 30 min, until the liquid is almost half.
  5. Add the brown sugar and stir.
  6. Lastly, add the bay leaves and plack pepper
  7. Taste the flavor of the chicken adobo, if too salty, too sweet or etc. add some water.
  8. Simmer again for about two minutes
  9. Serve hot and enjoy your chiken adobo!
