Mobile Legends

  Shanghai Moonton Technology, a game production firm based in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, created and distributed Mobile Legends, a multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) for iOS and Android smartphones.

History

How to play?

    - Mobile Legends pits two teams of five against one other, with the main goal of destroying the opponent's base.
    - Each player chooses a hero at the start of the game. Each hero has his or her own set of characteristics and abilities. In the game, there are 92 heroes divided into six categories: tank, fighter, assassin, marksman, support, and mage.
    - Players' obligations for their respective teams are determined by these roles. Support heroes, for example, have the ability to enhance or heal their teammates. That is why, in order to keep their comrades alive or strong in war, they need stick with them.
    - Players must also destroy enemy forces, notably computer-controlled minions, turrets, and heroes, in order to level up their heroes. They will be rewarded in-game if you do so.

Popularity of Mobile Legends

  Since its first release, the game has supposedly accumulated over 500 million downloads and 75 million active players. Southeast Asia, where the game has established itself as a significant esport, accounts for a large portion of its popularity. The MLBB Professional League, for example, is a Mobile Legends esports league in the Philippines. It just finished its fourth season, with Sunsparks taking home the championship and the majority of the $54,000 prize fund. Meanwhile, MLBB LIGA, a street fight event where fans compete for rewards and bragging rights, caters to non-professional players. The MLBB World Championship 2019, or M1, takes place outside of the country and comprises 16 professional teams from 14 different countries.
